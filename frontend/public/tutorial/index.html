Part 1: Guest Guide

Welcome! If you are a past graduate looking to reconnect with the IHCAE community, this guide explains how to navigate the public site and successfully register for an account.

1.1 Homepage & News

1.1.1 Exploring the Public Hub

When you arrive at the platform, you are greeted by the public homepage. This is your gateway to the community, featuring a welcoming hero banner and quick access to login or registration.

The main landing page for the IHCAE Alumni Network.

1.1.2 Public News & Events

Even without an account, you can stay up-to-date with the Institute. The News & Events section highlights public announcements, upcoming expeditions, and alumni gatherings published by the administration.

Public announcements and events visible to all visitors.

1.2 How to Register

To access the private directory and forums, you must have a verified alumni account. There are two ways to join the network.

1.2.1 Method 1: Email Invitation

If the administration has imported your legacy records, you may receive an automated email containing a secure setup link. Clicking this link allows you to bypass the approval process and instantly claim your digital profile.

1.2.2 Method 2: Self-Registration

If you haven't received an automatic link, you can manually register. Navigate to the Homepage and click the Register button.

You will need to input your details, such as which course you attended, the dates, and your contact information. It is crucial that you provide accurate information, as the admin team will use this to verify your identity against their physical records.

The registration form where you provide your course details for admin verification.

The Waiting Period (Pending Approval)

After submitting the form, your account enters a "Pending Approval" state. Because the admin team needs to double-check their records to confirm you actually took the course, you cannot log in immediately.

Once verified, you will receive a welcome email letting you know you can now log in!

1.2.3 Forgot Password?

If you have already registered but forgot your password, click the Login button on the homepage and select "Forgot Password". Enter your email address, and the system will send you a secure link to reset your credentials.

Part 2: Alumni Guide

Once your account is approved, you are ready to explore the private Alumni Portal. This section covers how to set up your profile and network with peers.

2.1 Dashboard & Profile

2.1.1 Your Personalized Dashboard

Upon successful authentication, you are greeted by your Alumni Dashboard. It acts as your central command center, providing a snapshot of recent activity, quick links to the directory, and tailored content based on your profile.

Dashboard Features

  • Live Statistics: See the total number of alumni, events, and active discussions at a glance.
  • Quick Feeds: View upcoming events and the latest active forum discussions.
  • My Submissions Tracking: Track any content you have submitted. You can see if it is Draft, Pending Review, Approved, or Rejected (including the admin's feedback).
The personalized Alumni Dashboard showing live feeds and submission tracking.

2.1.2 Managing Your Profile

Before connecting with others, it is highly recommended that you fill out your profile. Click on Profile in the navigation bar.

Click the Edit Profile button to upload a picture, update your personal information, add your current professional Location, and specify your Course and Batch. This data is critical because it allows other alumni to filter and find you in the directory.

The profile management screen showing the Edit Mode toggle.

2.2 Directory Search

The Directory is the core networking tool of the application. It allows you to search through the entire database of verified alumni.

You have powerful tools at your disposal to find peers and mentors:

  • Search Bar: Free text search to find specific names.
  • Filters: Dropdowns to narrow down peers by a specific Course or Graduation Year (Batch).
The searchable Alumni Directory with active filters.

2.3 Forums & Moderation

The Forums provide a private discussion board where you can ask questions, post job opportunities, or discuss industry trends.

2.3.1 Creating and Participating

To start a new discussion, click the New Thread button in the sidebar. This opens a modal where you can write your topic and optionally add tags to categorize it.

Creating a new discussion topic in the forums.

You can also participate in ongoing discussions by clicking into any topic and writing a reply to share your thoughts with the community.

Replying to an existing thread.

2.3.2 Reporting Inappropriate Content

To keep the community safe, every user has the power to moderate content.

Reporting Inappropriate Content

If you see spam or offensive material, click the small Flag icon on that post. This opens a modal where you can select a reason. The flagged post is immediately sent to the Administrators for review and potential removal.

Reporting a post for administrative review.

2.4 Submitting Content

The platform relies on user-generated content! If you have a success story to share or an announcement for the community, you can submit it directly through the portal.

Navigate to Submit Content. Fill out the title and description of your story. Note that your submission will not go live instantly. It is sent to an administrator for review.

You can track its status back on your Dashboard. Once the Admin reviews it, you will receive an automated email notification letting you know if it was approved and published.

The content submission form for alumni.

Once a success story is approved, it appears in the public Success Stories feed, where other alumni can read about your achievements.

The published Success Stories feed visible to the community.

Part 3: Admin Guide

Welcome to the IHCAE Console. As an Administrator, you have access to powerful tools to moderate the network. This section covers user management, CSV imports, and moderation workflows.

3.1 Console Overview

3.1.1 Navigating the Console

The sidebar on the left is divided into logical groups that dictate your workflow:

  • Dashboard: Your high-level overview of the entire system's health.
  • Users: Manage platform accounts, security roles, alumni approvals, and view the IHCAE master roster.
  • Content: Create news announcements and review user-submitted success stories.
  • Forums: Moderate discussion boards and handle flagged posts.

When you first log in, you land on the Dashboard. It provides four critical live statistics: Total Users, Pending, Approved, and Active Today. Below the statistics is a quick-access overview of the system.

The Admin Console showing the sidebar on the left and the Dashboard overview.

3.2 User Management

The Alumni & User Management section is the core of your administrative duties. Inside this section, you will find four horizontal sub-tabs: All Users, Alumni Directory, Pending Approvals, and IHCAE Records.

3.2.1 All Users Table

The first tab provides a comprehensive table of every registered account. You can filter users by their Role or search by name. Each row displays the user's status, whether their email is verified, and their assigned roles.

The master user management table showing all registered accounts.

Blocking Users

If an account is compromised, click the red Block (Ban) icon. A modal will prompt you to provide an optional reason.

When you choose to block a user, they will immediately be unable to log in to their account.

The Block User modal asking for a reason before taking action.

3.2.2 Editing Details & Passwords

Clicking the Edit Details icon on any user opens a powerful 3-tab modal. The first tab allows you to edit the user's First/Last name and assign administrative Roles.

Modifying a user's name and roles.

The third tab, Security & Password, allows you to force a password reset. You can either Send a Reset Link Email to the user, or manually Change Password Directly by typing a new one inline.

Manually overriding a user's password inline.

3.2.3 Admin Alumni Directory

The second sub-tab provides Admins with a master view of the searchable Alumni Directory. This is identical to what normal users see, but with the added ability to globally export all records as a CSV.

The Alumni Directory view within the Admin panel.

3.3 Pending Approvals

The third sub-tab is a dedicated queue strictly for reviewing self-registered accounts. When new users sign up, their accounts are placed in a holding state until an admin verifies them.

The Pending Approvals queue.

3.3.1 Reviewing User Details

To make an informed decision, click anywhere on a pending user's row. This expands an inline detail panel showing their submitted Course, Batch, Location, and Bio.

Clicking a row expands the user's submitted registration details.

3.3.2 Approving or Rejecting

Once you have reviewed the details, you can click Approve to instantly grant them access, or click Reject. Clicking Reject opens a confirmation modal where you can optionally provide a reason that will be emailed to the applicant.

The confirmation modal when an admin chooses to Reject an application.

3.4 IHCAE Records & Import

The final sub-tab is the IHCAE Records page. This acts as the authoritative database of valid alumni. Users who pre-exist in this database are automatically approved upon registration, bypassing the Pending queue. The table below shows the master roster before any records have been imported.

The IHCAE Records table before importing data.

3.4.1 Importing Legacy Records

You can quickly populate the database by uploading a CSV file containing past graduates. Click the Upload icon to select your CSV file. The system will parse it and present a Preview Modal where you can resolve any duplicate emails or formatting errors inline before finalizing.

The Import Preview modal showing the parsed CSV rows before committing to the database.

Once imported, the records populate the master roster table. They are marked as "Unclaimed" until the actual user registers or you manually generate an account for them.

The populated IHCAE master roster showing the newly imported records.

3.4.2 Bulk Account Generation

Instead of waiting for legacy alumni to find the portal, you can proactively create their accounts. Select multiple unclaimed rows and click Generate Accounts. This creates digital profiles for them and sends a secure setup link via email so they can claim their account.

The confirmation modal for bulk-generating digital accounts for imported records.

3.5 Content Moderation

The Content section allows you to manage the public-facing news feed and review community submissions.

3.5.1 Official Announcements

Use the News & Events tab to publish official announcements or schedule upcoming alumni gatherings. Content created here is immediately pushed to the public homepage and user Dashboards.

Managing official news and events from the admin panel.

3.5.2 Reviewing Success Stories

As covered in Part 2, normal alumni can submit success stories. These do not go live instantly. Instead, they appear in the Content Review tab.

The queue for reviewing user-submitted content.

Click the Review button on any pending submission to open the detailed review modal. Here you can read their entire draft, optionally attach an admin note, and choose to Approve or Reject the story.

The content review modal displaying a user's success story.

3.6 Forum Moderation

The Forums section gives you full visibility into all community discussions. It is divided into two main tabs: Topics and Flagged Content.

3.6.1 Viewing All Topics

The default Topics tab lists every thread created by alumni. You can search, sort by activity, and view the status of each topic (e.g., Pinned, Locked).

The Topics tab showing all active discussions across the network.

3.6.2 Managing Comments

To inspect a thread, click the View (eye) icon on any topic row. This opens the Topic Details modal, allowing you to read the entire conversation. If you spot an inappropriate comment, you can use the Delete (trash) icon on any specific post or reply to remove it instantly.

The Topic Details modal where admins can read the thread and delete specific comments.

3.6.3 Handling Flagged Content

When an alumni flags a post, it arrives in the Forum Moderation queue. Navigate to the Flagged Content tab to view these reports.

The Flagged Content tab listing all reported forum posts.

Click the Take Action button on any pending report to open the moderation console. Here, you can review the reporter's exact notes, read the offensive message, and apply disciplinary actions—such as formally warning the user, permanently deleting the post, or dismissing the flag as a false alarm.

The administration modal used to enforce community guidelines on flagged content.
