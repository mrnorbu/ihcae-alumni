🗣️ Forum Tutorials (Pinned & Locked)

Topic 1

Welcome to the IHCAE Alumni Hub! (Start Here)

Welcome to the official Indian Himalayan Centre for Adventure and Eco-Tourism (IHCAE) Alumni Network! This platform was built exclusively for past and present trainees to stay connected, share their mountain experiences, and collaborate on future expeditions. What can you do here? - Connect with your old batchmates from your Basic or Advance courses. - Stay updated on IHCAE news, upcoming refresher courses, and alumni meets. - Share your own expedition stories and success milestones. - Use these forums to ask technical mountaineering questions or form climbing teams. We are thrilled to have you here. Safe climbing!
Topic 2

How to use the Alumni Directory to find your Batchmates

The Alumni Directory is your most powerful tool for networking! 1. Navigate to the All Users tab. 2. Use the search bar to look up specific names. 3. Use the filters to find people by Course (e.g., Basic Mountaineering Course, Mountain Biking) or by Batch/Year. This makes it incredibly easy to find the people you trained with in the snow! If you can't find someone, invite them to join the platform.
Topic 3

Publishing your Expeditions and Stories

Did you recently summit a major peak? Or are you organizing a local trekking event? We want to feature it! If you have a success story or news you would like to share with the rest of the alumni network, please reach out to the platform administrators. You can send us the details of your expedition along with some high-quality photos, and we will review and publish it to the front page for everyone to see.
Topic 4

Setting up your Profile and Certifications

Your profile is your mountaineering resume! Make sure it represents you well. 1. Click on your name in the top right and go to Profile. 2. Upload a clear Profile Picture (preferably in your outdoor gear!). 3. Write a short bio about your current outdoor activities or profession. 4. Ensure your IHCAE Courses and Graduation Year are accurate so others can verify your training level. A complete profile helps build trust when you are forming teams for difficult expeditions.
Topic 5

Using the Forums: Best Practices

The Forums are open for all alumni to discuss gear, routes, weather, and team formations. Best Practices: - Search before posting: Someone might have already asked your question. - Be respectful: The mountains teach us humility, let's practice it here too. - Use clear titles: Instead of "Help", use "Need advice on gear for winter Elbrus climb". (Note: These tutorial threads are locked, but you are free to create new topics in the main forum page!)
