Chapter 1: Introduction & Roles

Welcome to the official documentation for the IHCAE Alumni App. This guide is designed to help you understand exactly how the platform operates, whether you are browsing as a guest, connecting as an alumnus, or managing the system as an administrator.

What is this platform?

The IHCAE Alumni App is a centralized digital hub designed to connect graduates from across the world. It provides a secure directory, private discussion forums, and a space to share success stories and upcoming events.

Understanding User Roles

To ensure security and data privacy, the platform operates on three distinct levels of access:

1. The Guest (Unauthenticated)

Anyone who visits the website without logging in is considered a Guest. They can view public announcements, read success stories on the homepage, and see upcoming events. However, they cannot view the private directory or post in the forums.

2. The Normal User (Verified Alumni)

Alumni who have registered and been manually verified by an administrator gain full access to the network. They can search the directory, update their professional profiles, register for private events, and participate in forum discussions.

3. The Administrator

Administrators are trusted staff members who manage the ecosystem. They are responsible for reviewing registrations, importing legacy CSV databases, moderating the forums, and creating official news and events.

Chapter 3: Registration & Joining the Network

Because the IHCAE Alumni App contains private information and a secure directory, you must have an approved account to access the network. There are two different ways you can get an account on the platform.

Method 1: The Automatic Link (Pre-Approved)

If you have already done a course with IHCAE and your previous details are in the physical alumni register, the administration team may have already added you to the system!

In this scenario, you don't need to fill out any forms. The Admin simply uploads the old records from the back-end. The system will then send you an automatic email invitation.

  • Check your inbox for an email from the IHCAE Alumni Network.
  • Click the secure link inside the email.
  • You will be asked to create your own private password. Once saved, you are instantly logged in!

Method 2: Self-Registration

If you haven't received an automatic link, you can manually register yourself. To begin, navigate to the Homepage and click the Register button in the top right corner.

You will need to input your details, such as which course you did, the dates you attended, and your contact information. It is crucial that you provide accurate information, as the admin team will use this to verify who you are.

Figure 3.1: The registration form where you provide your course details.

The Waiting Period (Pending Approval)

After you submit the form, your account is placed into a "Pending Approval" state. Because the admin team needs to double-check their records to confirm you actually took the course, you cannot log in immediately.

Once the admin verifies your details and approves your account from their dashboard, you will receive a welcome email letting you know you can now log in!

Chapter 4: Dashboard & Profile

Once an administrator has approved your account, or if you received an email invitation, you are ready to explore the private Alumni Portal.

Logging In & The Dashboard

Click Login from the Homepage and enter your email and password. Upon successful authentication, you are greeted by your personalized Alumni Dashboard.

The dashboard acts as your central command center. It provides a snapshot of recent activity, quick links to the directory, and tailored content based on your profile.

Dashboard Features

  • Live Statistics: See the total number of alumni, events, and active discussions at a glance.
  • Quick Feeds: View upcoming events (with dates and locations) and the latest active forum discussions.
  • My Submissions Tracking: Track any content you have submitted. You can see if it is Draft, Pending Review, Approved, or Rejected (including the admin's feedback so you can Edit and resubmit it).

Email Notifications

The IHCAE platform keeps you connected even when you are logged out via automated email notifications. You will receive an email when:

  • Your initial registration is approved by an administrator.
  • Your submitted news or success stories are approved or rejected.
  • You request a password reset.

Make sure to check your spam folder and mark IHCAE emails as "Not Spam" to ensure you never miss an update!

Figure 4.1: The personalized Alumni Dashboard showing live feeds and submission tracking.

Managing Your Profile

Before connecting with others, it's highly recommended to fill out your profile. Click on Profile in the navigation bar.

By default, you are in View Mode. Click the Edit Profile button to enter Edit Mode. Here, you can upload a profile picture, update your personal information, add your current professional Location, and specify your Course and Batch. This data is critical because it allows other alumni to filter and find you in the directory.

Figure 4.2: The profile management screen showing the Edit Mode toggle.

You can track its status back on your Dashboard. Once the Admin reviews it, you will receive an automated email notification letting you know if it was approved and published, or if it requires edits.

Chapter 8: The Admin Console & Dashboard

Welcome to the IHCAE Console. As an Administrator, you have access to powerful tools to moderate the network. Let's first look at your primary navigation tool: the Admin Sidebar located on the left.

  • Dashboard: Your high-level overview of the entire system's health.
  • Users: Manage platform accounts, security roles, alumni approvals, and view the IHCAE master roster.
  • Content: Create news announcements and review user-submitted success stories.
  • Forums: Moderate discussion boards and handle flagged posts.

The Dashboard Overview

When you first log in, you land on the Dashboard. It provides four critical live statistics: Total Users, Pending, Approved, and Active Today. Below the statistics is a quick-access overview of the system.

Chapter 9: User Management

The Alumni & User Management section is the core of your administrative duties. Inside this section, you will find four horizontal sub-tabs: All Users, Alumni Directory, Pending Approvals, and IHCAE Records.

9.1 All Users Table

The first tab provides a comprehensive table of every registered account. You can filter users by their Role or search by name. Each row displays the user's status, whether their email is verified, and their assigned roles.

Figure 9.1: The master user management table.

Blocking Users

If an account is compromised, click the red Block (Ban) icon. A modal will prompt you to provide an optional reason.

When you choose to block a user, they will immediately be unable to log in to their account.

Figure 9.2: The Block User modal asking for a reason.

Editing & Passwords

Clicking the Edit Details icon on any user opens a powerful 3-tab modal. The first tab allows you to edit the user's First/Last name and assign administrative Roles.

Figure 9.3: Modifying a user's name and roles.

The third tab, Security & Password, allows you to force a password reset. You can either Send a Reset Link Email to the user, or manually Change Password Directly by typing a new one inline.

Figure 9.4: Manually overriding a user's password inline.

9.2 Alumni Directory

The second sub-tab provides Admins with a master view of the searchable Alumni Directory. This is identical to what normal users see, but with the added ability to globally export all records as a CSV.

Figure 9.5: The Alumni Directory view within the Admin panel.

9.3 Pending Approvals

The third sub-tab is a dedicated queue strictly for reviewing self-registered accounts. When new users sign up, their accounts are placed in a holding state until an admin verifies them.

Figure 9.6: The Pending Approvals queue.

Reviewing User Details

To make an informed decision, click anywhere on a pending user's row. This expands an inline detail panel showing their submitted Course, Batch, Location, and Bio.

Figure 9.7: Clicking a row expands the user's submitted registration details.

Approving or Rejecting

Once you have reviewed the details, you can click Approve to instantly grant them access, or click Reject. Clicking Reject opens a confirmation modal where you can optionally provide a reason that will be emailed to the applicant.

Figure 9.8: The confirmation modal when an admin chooses to Reject an application.

9.4 IHCAE Records & CSV Import

The final sub-tab is the IHCAE Records page. This acts as the authoritative database of valid alumni. Users who pre-exist in this database are automatically approved upon registration, bypassing the Pending queue. The table below shows the master roster before any records have been imported.

Figure 9.9: The IHCAE Records table before importing data.

Importing Legacy Records

You can quickly populate the database by uploading a CSV file containing past graduates. Click the Upload icon to select your CSV file. The system will parse it and present a Preview Modal where you can resolve any duplicate emails or formatting errors inline before finalizing.

Figure 9.10: The Import Preview modal showing the parsed CSV rows before committing to the database.

Once imported, the records populate the master roster table. They are marked as "Unclaimed" until the actual user registers or you manually generate an account for them.

Figure 9.11: The populated IHCAE master roster showing the newly imported records.

Bulk Account Generation

Instead of waiting for legacy alumni to find the portal, you can proactively create their accounts. Select multiple unclaimed rows and click Generate Accounts. This creates digital profiles for them and sends a secure setup link via email so they can claim their account.

Figure 9.11: The confirmation modal for bulk-generating digital accounts for imported records.

When an alumni flags a post, it arrives in the Forum Moderation queue. To view these reports, navigate to the Flagged Content tab.

Click the Take Action button on any pending report to open the moderation console. Here, you can review the reporter's exact notes, read the offensive message, and apply disciplinary actions—such as formally warning the user, permanently deleting the post, or dismissing the flag as a false alarm.
